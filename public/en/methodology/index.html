Sage Scroll

Source grades and methodology

This map presents lives, works, and places with their source links. A linked source and a verified claim are not treated as the same thing.

Source grades

A · Public or official record

Primary material from public institutions, recognised archives, museums, libraries, or comparable official records.

B · Scholarly or accountable editorial source

Material from academic institutions, scholarly publications, or an editorially accountable source. Each item links to the underlying source where available.

C · Source needs expansion

A Wikipedia-only or otherwise insufficiently cross-checked reference. It is labelled as needing source expansion and is excluded from the verified-profile count.

Verified count

The “Verified A·B” total includes only profiles explicitly marked verified and connected to an A or B source. A link alone does not automatically verify a profile, and C-grade entries are not counted.

What to check on each profile

  • “Source” links beside a timeline event, work, or place lead to material for that fact.
  • Known uncertainty, such as disputed birthplaces or unverified coordinates, is displayed separately.
  • Shopping links are separate from factual sources and do not affect grades or verification.

Scope and limits

Sage Scroll is a guide to factual source material, not a complete biography or an assessment of a person. Sources can change and new records can emerge, so individual grades require ongoing review.
