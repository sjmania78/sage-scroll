Han Yong-un

한용운
Poet, Buddhist monk, and independence activist · 1879–1944
Explore places on the map

Life

  1. 1879Born in Hongseong, Chungnam (August 29). Real name Jeong-ok, Cheongju clansource
  2. 1905Attained enlightenment at Baekdamsa Temple on Seoraksan Mountain; Dharma name Yong-un (龍雲)source
  3. 1910Wrote 'On the Reformation of Korean Buddhism' (Joseon Bulgyo Yusillon)source
  4. 1919Participated as one of the 33 National Representatives of the March 1st Movement (representing the Buddhist community) and signed the Declaration of Independencesource
  5. 1925Completed the poem 'The Silence of My Beloved' (Nimui Chimmuk) (August 29)source
  6. 1926Published the poetry collection 'The Silence of My Beloved' (Hoedong Seogwan)source
  7. 1927Participated in forming the Singanhoe, serving as a central executive committee member and head of the Gyeongseong branchsource
  8. 1944Died at Simujang in Seongbuk-dong, Seoul (June 29)source
