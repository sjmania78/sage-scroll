Ibn Battuta

Traveler, explorer and Islamic jurist · 1304–1369

Life

  1. 1304Born in Tangier under the Marinid dynasty into a family of qadis (Islamic judges)source
  2. 1325At age 21 set out alone from Tangier for the pilgrimage to Mecca, beginning roughly 30 years of travelsource
  3. 1334Arrived in Delhi and was appointed qadi under the sultan, serving about eight yearssource
  4. 1341Set out from Delhi as the sultan's envoy to the emperor of Chinasource
  5. 1352Crossed the Sahara and spent about a year in the Mali Empire at its heightsource
  6. 1354Returned to Morocco and dictated his travels to the scribe Ibn Juzayysource
  7. 1369Died in Morocco (year uncertain); Tangier is credited as his burial placesource

Full title 'A Gift to Those Who Contemplate the Wonders of Cities and the Marvels of Travelling.' Records some 120,000 km of travel over about 30 years, dictated late in life to the scribe Ibn Juzayy
