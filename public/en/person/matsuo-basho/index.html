Matsuo Bashō

마쓰오 바쇼
Haiku poet · 1644–1694
Explore places on the map

Life

  1. 1644Born in Ueno, Iga Province (present-day Iga, Mie Prefecture)source
  2. 1672Moved to Edo (present-day Tokyo) to study poetrysource
  3. 1680Built a hermitage in Fukagawa and planted a banana plant (bashō); from this time he used the pen name 'Bashō'source
  4. 1689Traveled on foot through the Tōhoku region for about 150 days (roughly 2,400 km) together with his disciple Kawai Sorasource
  5. 1694Died of illness in Osaka (November 28)source

Works

'Furu ike ya' (The Old Pond) 1686

A standalone haiku composed at his Edo hermitage. The most widely known of all Japanese haiku.

Verify source

The Narrow Road to the Deep North (奥の細道) 1689

A travel account based on his 1689 journey through the Tōhoku region. It combines verse and prose, and was published posthumously.
