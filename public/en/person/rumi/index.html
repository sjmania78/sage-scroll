Jalal al-Din Rumi

잘랄 알딘 루미
Poet and Sufi mystic · 1207–1273
Explore places on the map

Life

  1. 1207Born near Balkh (present-day Afghanistan) or in Vakhsh (present-day Tajikistan); son of the mystic theologian Baha al-Din Waladsource
  2. 1218Family left Balkh amid the Mongol threat, traveling via a pilgrimage to Mecca and through the Middle Eastsource
  3. 1228Invited to settle in Konya, capital of the Seljuq Sultanate of Rumsource
  4. 1244Met the wandering dervish Shams al-Din of Tabriz in Konya, transforming from jurist to mystic poetsource
  5. 1258Began dictating his didactic epic the Masnavi, continuing until his death (left unfinished)source
  6. 1273Died on 17 December in Konya; buried next to his father, his shrine becoming a place of pilgrimagesource

Works

Masnavi (Masnavi-ye Ma'navi)

A six-book Persian didactic epic of some 25,000 verses that broadly influenced mystical thought and literature across the Muslim world
