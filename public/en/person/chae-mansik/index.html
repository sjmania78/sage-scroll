Chae Man-sik

채만식
Novelist and playwright · 1902–1950
Explore places on the map

Life

  1. 1902Born in Eumnae-ri, Impi-myeon, Okgu-gun, North Jeolla Provincesource
  2. 1918Entered Jungang High Schoolsource
  3. 1922Graduated from Jungang High School and entered the literature department of the First Waseda Higher School attached to Waseda University in Japansource
  4. 1924Dropped out of Waseda Higher School and published the short story 'Segillo (Three Roads)' in Joseon Mundan (literary debut)source
  5. 1925Joined the Dong-a Ilbo companysource
  6. 1929Joined the Gaebyeoksa companysource
  7. 1934Published the short story 'A Ready-Made Life' in Sin Dongasource
  8. 1937Began serializing the novel 'Muddy Currents' in the Chosun Ilbo (Oct 12, 1937 – May 17, 1938)source
  9. 1938Serialized the novel 'Cheonha Taepyeongchun' in Chogwang and published the short story 'The Idiot Uncle' in the Dong-a Ilbosource
  10. 1948Republished 'Cheonha Taepyeongchun' in book form under the new title 'Peace Under Heaven' through Dongjisasource
  11. 1950Died of tuberculosis in Iri (present-day Iksan), North Jeolla Province (aged 49)source

Works

Muddy Currents (탁류) 1937

Serialized in the Chosun Ilbo Oct 12, 1937 – May 17, 1938; published in book form by Bangmun Seogwan in 1939. Set in Gunsan.

Verify source

Peace Under Heaven (태평천하) 1938

When serialized in Chogwang in 1938 it was titled 'Cheonha Taepyeongchun'; it was retitled 'Taepyeongcheonha' in the 1948 Dongjisa book edition. A novel dealing with the family of the landlord Yun Jik-won.

Verify source

A Ready-Made Life (레디메이드 인생) 1934

Short story serialized in three parts in Sin Donga, May–July 1934.

Verify source

The Idiot Uncle (치숙) 1938

Short story published in the Dong-a Ilbo, March 7–14, 1938. Uses a first-person narrator technique.
