Kim Whan-ki

김환기
Western-style painter · 1913–1974
Explore places on the map

Life

  1. 1913Born in Eupdong-ri, Anjwa-myeon, Sinan-gun, South Jeolla Province (April 13, 1913)source
  2. 1934While studying in Japan, took part in the Avant-Garde Art Institutesource
  3. 1936Graduated from the art department of Nihon University in Tokyo and completed its graduate research coursesource
  4. 1937Organized the Baengmanhoe; returned to Korea around this timesource
  5. 1948Appointed professor at the College of Fine Arts of Seoul National University; organized the Sinsasilpa (New Realists)source
  6. 1952Appointed professor at Hongik Universitysource
  7. 1956Went to Paris and stayed there for about three years, working on his artsource
  8. 1963Participated in the São Paulo Biennale as Korea's representativesource
  9. 1965Moved to New York; received an honorary prize in the painting section of the São Paulo Biennalesource
  10. 1974Died in New York, United States (July 25, 1974)source
  11. 1992His bereaved family (his wife Kim Hyang-an and the Whanki Foundation) opened the Whanki Museum in Buam-dong, Jongno-gu, Seoul (November 5, 1992)source

Where, in What Form, Shall We Meet Again (Eodiseo Mueosi Doeeo Dasi Mannara) 1970

Oil on canvas, 236×172 cm. The title is quoted from the last line of Kim Kwang-seop's poem 'In the Evening.' A full-surface dot painting from his New York period.
