Yun Seon-do

윤선도
Sijo poet and civil official · 1587–1671
Explore places on the map

Life

  1. 1587Born in Yeonhwa-bang, Eastern District, Hanseongsource
  2. 1616Submitted the Byeongjin Memorial impeaching powerful ministers and was exiled to Gyeongwon in Hamgyeong Provincesource
  3. 1628Placed first in the preliminary state civil-service examination and became tutor to Princes Bongnim and Inpyeongsource
  4. 1637After the Manchu invasion of 1636, went into seclusion at Buyongdong on Bogil Islandsource
  5. 1642Composed the 18 poems of 'Sanjung Singok' (including 'Song of Five Friends') at Geumswae-dongsource
  6. 1651Composed the 40 poems of 'Eobusasisa (Fisherman's Calendar)' at Buyongdong on Bogil Islandsource
  7. 1659Exiled to Samsu in Hamgyeong Province in connection with the Gihae Ritual Controversysource
  8. 1671Died at Nakseojae in Buyongdong on Bogil Island at the age of 85source

Works

Song of Five Friends (오우가) 1642

A linked sijo sequence of six poems included in 'Sanjung Singok.' It sings of water, stone, pine, bamboo, and the moon as five friends.

Eobusasisa (Fisherman's Calendar) (어부사시사) 1651

A linked sijo sequence of 40 poems—10 each for spring, summer, autumn, and winter—composed with Buyongdong on Bogil Island as its setting.
