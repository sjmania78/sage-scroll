Socrates

소크라테스
Philosopher · 470 BCE–399 BCE
Explore places on the map

Life

  1. 470 BCEBorn in the Athenian deme of Alopece, son of Sophroniscus (a stonemason) and Phaenarete (a midwife).source
  2. 432 BCEServed as a hoplite at the Battle of Potidaea, where he was said to have saved the wounded Alcibiades.source
  3. 424 BCEFought at the Battle of Delium, praised for his composure during the Athenian retreat.source
  4. 406 BCEAs a member of the Boule, opposed the unlawful collective trial of the generals after the Battle of Arginusae.source
  5. 399 BCETried on charges of impiety (asebeia) and corrupting the youth, brought by Meletus, Anytus, and Lycon.source
  6. 399 BCEFound guilty, sentenced to death, and executed by drinking hemlock.source

Works

Apology (recorded by Plato)

Socrates wrote nothing himself; his thought survives mainly through his pupils Plato and Xenophon. The Apology is Plato's account of Socrates' defense speech at his trial.
