Life

  1. 1536Born at his maternal family's home (Ojukheon) in Gangneung; his mother was Sin Saimdangsource
  2. 1548Passed the preliminary jinsa examination at age 13source
  3. 1558Took first place with his essay 'Cheondochaek (天道策)' in a special examinationsource
  4. 1564Passed the civil service examination (taking first place on multiple occasions, earning him the title 'Gudojangwongong'); began his official career as Hojo jwarangsource
  5. 1569Wrote 'Dongho mundap (東湖問答)'source
  6. 1574Submitted the 'Maneon bongsa (萬言封事)' while serving as Ubu-seungjisource
  7. 1575Presented the 'Seonghak jibyo (聖學輯要)' to King Seonjosource
  8. 1577Wrote 'Gyeongmong yogyeol (擊蒙要訣)' while serving in Haejusource
  9. 1582Appointed Minister of Personnel (Ijo panseo)source
  10. 1583Appointed Minister of War (Byeongjo panseo)source
  11. 1584Died in Daesa-dong, Hanyang (aged 49)source

Works

Seonghak Jibyo (聖學輯要, Essentials of the Learning of the Sages) 1575

A textbook on the learning of rulership presented to King Seonjo in 1575. It summarizes the essentials of the Four Books and Six Classics.

Gyeongmong Yogyeol (擊蒙要訣, Essentials for Dispelling Ignorance) 1577

A primer for beginning students, written in 1577 while serving in Haeju. The autograph manuscript is held at the Ojukheon Museum in Gangneung.

Dongho Mundap (東湖問答, Dongho Dialogues) 1569

A treatise on statecraft in dialogue form, written in 1569 during his sabbatical study leave at the Dongho Reading Hall.
